Biography

  • By Mark Jones
  • at

Mark Jones began his writing career in Saturday morning cartoons, selling his first script to Filmation Studios. From there, he went on-staff at DePatie-Freleng, where he was mentored by the famed Warner Bros cartoon director, Friz Freleng, developing animated series and writing the Saturday Morning “Mister Magoo” cartoon show for CBS, and the “Pink Panther” show for NBC.

Jones was then hired by Joseph Barbera to develop and write for Hanna-Barbera Studios’ Saturday morning line-up. He Story Edited and wrote “Captain Caveman and the Teen Angels” for ABC, along with “Super Friends”, “Scooby Doo”, “Scrappy Doo”, “All New Popeye Hour”, “Dyno-Mutt, Dog Wonder” and “Robotic Stooges”, along with many other Hanna-Barbera Saturday Morning series.

Mark was hired by Joe Ruby and Ken Spears for their newly created studio, Ruby-Spears, to write the pilot for their first animated series, “FangFace”, which was picked up by ABC. Jones stayed on as head of development, where he developed and wrote the pilots for the ABC series, “Mighty Man and Yukk” and “Rickety Rocket”. Mark developed and wrote the pilots, and produced the ABC series, “The Puppy’s New Adventures”, “Rubic, The Amazing Cube”, the NBC “Mister T Cartoon Show”, along with developing and writing the pilot for “Turbo-Teen”, the only original Saturday morning animated show to be picked up by any of the three networks that season. Jones also wrote the pilots for the syndicated cartoon shows, “Mega Man” and “Sky Surfer Strike Force”.

Jones continued working in animation, story editing and writing for dozens of cartoon shows, including “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles”, “James Bond Jr.”, Plastic Man”, “Heathcliff”, “Marmaduke”, “Wild West C.O.W. Boys of Moo Mesa”, “Alf”, “Mork and Mindy” and “Police Academy: The Series”, among dozens of others.

Mark was brought into prime time live-action by Glen A. Larson and Universal Studios, where he wrote and story edited numerous television series, including “B.J. and the Bear”, “Sheriff Lobo”, “Galactica 1980” and “Misfits of Science”, along with many series for other studios, including “The Fall Guy”, “Werewolf”, “Nightman” and “Powers of Matthew Star”, among others.

Stephen J. Cannell then brought Jones on staff, where he wrote and story edited on “The A-Team”, “Hunter”, “The Rousters” and “Riptide”. Mark also developed live-action series and wrote pilots for Universal, Disney and Fred Silverman, producing the TV shows “Knight Rider”, “High Tide” and “Highwayman”, and was the show runner for the successful syndicated live-action “Superboy” TV series.

Jones kept his hand in animation and sold two original animated series concepts to Steven Spielberg, when Mr. Spielberg began his foray into TV animation.

Mark went on to write features for Universal, Tri-Star and wrote the first draft of the new incarnation of “Superman” for Alexander and Ilya Salkind and Warner Bros.

Jones’ feature writing and directing debut came with the successful horror feature, “Leprechaun”, starring Jennifer Aniston, which spawned five sequels and a new re-boot. Jones continued to write and direct features, including the theatrical feature, “Rumpelstiltskin”, for Dino DeLaurentiis, “Triloquist”, released by the Weinstein Company, and his latest feature thriller, “Scorned”, released by Anchor Bay Entertainment. Mark is currently in development on new productions for animation, television and film.
